# Week 12 Group B Activity — Slide Deck Source

This file is the source script for the Week 12 activity slide deck.

## Slide 1 — Title
**EDU 442 — Week 12 Group B Activity**  
**Disability, DisCrit, Representation, and Practice**

Subtitle: Discussion protocol for Monday session

---

## Slide 2 — Goals and flow
### Goals
- Connect key themes from the readings to real classroom scenarios.
- Get everyone involved through warm-up, pair/share, and board-based collaboration.
- Move from personal examples to reading-based analysis and practice implications.

### Flow
- Welcome and framing
- Warm-up: Quote Sort
- Pair and Share with discussant questions
- Hexagonal Thinking on the boards
- Share-out and synthesis
- Reflection and norms check-in

---

## Slide 3 — Warm-up: Quote Sort
### Instructions
- Each table gets printed quotes from the readings.
- Sort them into two categories:
  - "We agree / this resonates"
  - "We want to push back / this challenges us"
- Choose one quote from each pile to share out briefly.

### Purpose
Start with students' own reactions and lived examples before moving into theory.

---

## Slide 4 — Pair and Share
### Instructions
- Everyone reads all 7 discussant questions.
- Each person chooses 1–2 questions that resonate most.
- Find a partner or small group.
- Discuss for 5–7 minutes.
- Bring one idea from that discussion into the board activity.

### Reminder
Students do not need to stick to the question assigned to a specific discussant.

## Slide 7 — Hexagonal Thinking on the boards
### What students do
- Each board has one printed page with a center quote and 6 surrounding concept hexagons.
- Students do not get pre-drawn lines.
- They draw the lines themselves and explain why two ideas belong together.
- Groups should identify:
  - one strong connection
  - one debatable connection
  - one implication for educators

---

## Slide 8 — Board pages: the 8 center prompts
1. Race and disability are co-constructed through schooling.
2. "That's for the special education audience."
3. The affirmative non-tragedy model.
4. Disability is not tragedy.
5. Many people come to understand disabilities from what they have seen on television.
6. "Become more encompassing of diversity and perceived difference."
7. "Teachers struggled with that fine line ..."
8. Care, competence, risk, and protection.

---

## Slide 9 — Debrief and close
### Share-out
- Share one connection your group felt most confident about.
- Share one connection your group debated.

### Reflection
Complete: "After today's reading and discussion, I'm now thinking differently about _______."

### Norms check
Did we get everyone involved? What would we do differently next time?

---

## Slide 10 — Materials and facilitator reminders
### Materials
- Printed board pages
- Discussant questions on screen
- Markers and board space
- Visible timer
- Phone/camera for documentation

### Facilitator reminders
- Keep timing tight.
- Invite quieter voices.
- Push groups to explain the reasoning behind each connection.
