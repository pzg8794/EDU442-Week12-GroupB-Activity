## SIMPLE BOARD EXAMPLE
```text

      [Hexagon 5] -------- [Hexagon 4] -------- [Hexagon 8]
            \                   |                    /
             \                  |                   /
              \             [Hexagon 6]           /
               \                |                /
                \               |               /
                 [Hexagon 1] ---+--- [Hexagon 2]
                                 \
                                  \
                               [Hexagon 7]

```

### How to use the board
- Put one quote/phrase in each hexagon.
- Spread them out across the board.
- Let students draw the lines.
- Ask them to justify why two hexagons should connect.

---

## FAST VERSION FOR THE BOARD
If you do not want the full quote in the hexagon, use these shorter forms:

1. **Race + disability are co-constructed**
2. **"For the special education audience"**
3. **Affirmative non-tragedy model**
4. **Disability is not tragedy**
5. **TV teaches disability**
6. **More encompassing of difference**
7. **The fine line: support vs over-helping**
8. **Care, competence, risk, protection**
